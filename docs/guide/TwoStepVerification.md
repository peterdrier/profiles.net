# Two-step verification (2FA)

Two-step verification — sometimes called **2FA** or **2-Step Verification** — is
just one extra check when you sign in. After your password, it asks for a second
thing that only *you* have: usually a tap or a number on your phone. That way, if
someone ever guessed or stole your password, they still couldn't get in without
your phone in their hand.

It's the single biggest thing that keeps an account safe, which is why we turn it
on for everyone.

## What it applies to

This is about your **`@nobodies.team` Google account** — your org email and
anything Google (Gmail, shared drives, calendars). It is **required** on that
account, and you'll be walked through setting it up the very first time you sign
in to it.

It is **not** about getting into the Humans app itself. You get into the app with
a one-time login link or by clicking "sign in with Google" — there's no separate
password to protect there. (More on that in
[Signing in & getting unstuck](SigningIn.md).) So when we talk about 2FA, we mean
your `@nobodies.team` email account.

## Setting it up

Most people set it up during their first sign-in, because Google prompts you. If
you skipped it or want to check it:

1. Sign in to your `@nobodies.team` account at
   [myaccount.google.com](https://myaccount.google.com).
2. Open **Security** on the left.
3. Find **2-Step Verification** and follow the steps.

When it asks how you'd like to do the second step, the two easiest choices are:

- **A prompt on your phone** — Google sends a "Is this you? Yes/No" tap to a
  phone you're already signed in on. Nothing to type.
- **An authenticator app** — a free app (Google Authenticator, Authy, and
  others) that shows a 6-digit code that changes every 30 seconds.

Either is fine. Pick whichever feels less fiddly to you.

## Always set up a backup

Phones get lost, replaced, and dropped in the desert. Set up at least one backup
*now*, while you're already in there, so a dead phone never locks you out:

- **Backup codes** — Google gives you a list of one-time codes. Save them
  somewhere safe (write them down, or keep them in a password manager). Each one
  gets you in once if your phone isn't available.
- **A second method** — e.g. a second phone, or a partner's phone as a backup
  prompt.

## If you get locked out

Don't panic, and don't make a second account.

1. If you saved **backup codes**, use one of those to get in.
2. No backup codes and no phone? Email
   [humans@nobodies.team](mailto:humans@nobodies.team) or use the help button in
   the app (the round button, bottom-right). An admin can help sort out your
   `@nobodies.team` account from the Google side.

## Quick answers

| If you're wondering… | Here's the answer |
|---|---|
| Do I really have to? | Yes, on your `@nobodies.team` account — it's required and it's quick |
| Does this change how I get into the app? | No. The app login is separate — see [Signing in & getting unstuck](SigningIn.md) |
| What if I don't have a smartphone? | Backup codes work without a smartphone, and a phone that receives texts can also be used. If you're stuck, email [humans@nobodies.team](mailto:humans@nobodies.team) |
| I got a new phone | Sign in with a backup code, then add the new phone under Security → 2-Step Verification. If you're locked out, email [humans@nobodies.team](mailto:humans@nobodies.team) |
| Is it on my personal Gmail too? | Only if you turn it on there yourself — which is a good idea, but separate from your `@nobodies.team` account |
